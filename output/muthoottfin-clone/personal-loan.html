What is a personal loan?

A personal loan comes in handy when you have an immediate financial requirement with minimum turnaround time& it is also known as an ‘all-purpose loan’. Unlike other secured loan products a personal loan has easy eligibility criteria & documentation. Today, one can avail instant personal loans to fulfil their personal needs.

How to get a personal loan?

If you are in need of immediate credit, the first thing you need to do is submit a personal loan application form along with applicable documents to the lender which is then verified and approved. After this, the loan amount is disbursed into your bank account. Upon receiving the loan amount, you are required to repay the lender through EMIs (Equated monthly instalments) over the course of the loan repayment tenure.

A personal loan, if used judiciously, can also prove to be advantageous for improving your credit score.

Why choose a personal loan?

A personal loan can help you take care of various financial needs in a convenient manner. Major expenses can come up at any time without warning and could drain your savings. In such situations, opting for a personal loan can be very helpful. Some of the common reasons for applying for a personal loan can include financing a festive celebration, constructing a house, fixing a car, etc.

In addition, a personal loan can also aid you in meeting any shortfall experienced in purchasing a car or house, or in cases of medical contingencies, or for the higher education of your children, among other things. Going for a personal loan offers the easiest way to raise funds to make all your future plans come true, that too in a quick manner.

Let’s explore some of the other reasons why one can opt for a personal loan:

  • Debt Consolidation: It aids you in consolidating your expenses as you can pay off your credit card bills or other debts on time.
  • Flexibility of Use: you have the flexibility to use the loan for any expense
  • Quick Approval: At the time of securing a personal loan, minimum documentation is required, which results in quicker processing.
  • Reasonable Interest Rates: Another reason to go for personal loans is that they are available at reasonable interest rates.

All in all, numerous people are opting for instant personal loans today for many different reasons and you can avail one too if you have a personal need to take care of.

Factors That Affect Personal Loan Eligibility

Before you apply for a personal loan online, you should know about the different factors that impact your eligibility for a personal loan. These include:

  • Geographic location: The area where you live plays an important role in determining your eligibility for a personal loan.
  • Existing credit: If you are already repaying other loans your eligibility for a new loan will be affected.
  • Housing situation: If you live in your own house, you have an increased chance of getting your personal loan application approved. This is because if you reside in a rental house, it reduces your disposable income, which will lead to lower eligibility of Personal loan.
  • Income: The higher a person’s income, the higher is the loan amount they are eligible for. So, it may be said that your personal loan eligibility also depends on your income.
  • Organisation: Another determining factor in your personal loan eligibility is the company for which you work. Working for a well-known company that holds a good reputation in the market ensures that you have a stable job.
  • Credit history: Your credit history and credit score have the largest impact on your personal loan eligibility. The tenure, the interest rate, and the total loan amount that you can borrow will depend on this.

If individuals want to improve their eligibility for personal loans, they should pay their loan EMIs and credit card dues on time and in full, decrease their existing debt, minimising credit utilization, and so on.

Factors That Impact Interest Rates On Personal Loans

Another thing that you should be aware of before you apply for a personal loan online is the interest rate and the factors that affect it.

  • Income : At the time of deciding the interest rate, loan providers consider the income of the applicant. People with high income may be offered a lower interest rate. Individuals with lower annual incomes, on the other hand, may have to pay an interest rate that is higher.
  • Nature of the employment: Interest rates offered may differ on the basis of whether the applicant is Salaried profile with regular employment.
  • Employer details: There is an increased chance of a financial institution offering you a lower rate of interest if you work at a reputed organization.

How To Get Lower Interest Rates On A Personal Loan?

When you are about to apply for a personal loan online, there are certain tips that can prove to be helpful in availing a lower rate of interest. Some of these tips include:

  • Improve Your Credit Score: A high credit score denotes that an individual is creditworthy. A lender will offer lower personal loan interest rates to an applicant with a high credit score. So, before you apply for a personal loan online, you should check your credit score.
  • Do Not Miss Repayments: If you miss a credit card or a loan repayment, your credit score gets affected in an adverse manner. Loan providers generally consider your repayment history before deciding the interest rate for a personal loan. Individuals who have paid their past EMIs as well as credit card bills on time are more likely to be charged a lower interest rate.
  • Look out for offers: During festive occasions, financial institutions generally offer special interest rates for a limited period of time. You may be offered a lower interest rate if you apply for a loan when such offers are available.

Things To Know Before Applying For A Personal Loan

Some important points that you should be aware of before applying for a personal loan are:

  • Analyse the necessity of loan: The purpose of taking personal loans or any other loan for that matter is generally to meet immediate financial needs. So, if you can handle your financial situation without availing the loan, you must restrain from getting it.
  • Penalty Charges: If you don’t pay your EMIs/dues on time, a high penalty will be charged.
  • Borrow within your means: When you apply for a personal loan, ensure you borrow within your means. An individual should choose to go for a personal loan in a careful manner after carrying out due research. You should also understand both the benefits and costs involved before availing a personal loan.

Is there any Balance Transfer offer on a personal loan? Are there any benefits of this balance transfer?

Yes, you have the option of a balance transfer when you take a personal loan. A Personal Loan balance transfer is the procedure by which a borrower moves the whole outstanding personal Loan amount from one bank to another. Typically, it occurs when the new bank offers a reduced interest rate on the outstanding loan balance. Therefore, the main goal of a personal loan transfer is to lessen the total amount of debt.

The primary benefit of a personal loan balance transfer is to lessen the borrower's interest burden. Moreover, you can opt for a lender that offers top-up loans at competitive rates of interest to ensure that you do not have to face any financial constraints.

Are there any tax benefits associated with personal loans?

Personal loans do provide tax advantages depending on how the loan is used. The Indian Income Tax Act permits tax deductions for loans taken out for particular objectives, such as education, home improvement or purchase, business expansion, etc.

Property construction: According to Section 24 of the IT Act, if the loan amount is utilised to buy or build a home, the interest paid can be deducted from your taxable income.

Business expansion or purchase of assets: If the loan money is used for any other business purpose or to acquire assets other than real estate, the interest paid is added to the acquisition cost. This will reduce the capital gains and, in return, the tax liability.

What do the terms settlement, default and closed mean? Does it affect my credit score?

Loan closed/closure: A loan is marked close when the borrower pays all the loan amount (principal amount + interest) in a timely manner without any failure.

Loan settlement: It refers to a situation of obtaining a loan from a bank or NBFC, but you are unable to make payments owing to a sickness, an injury, a loss of employment, or another reason. In this case, you notify the lender of your position and request that you be given some time off before beginning repayments. The lender may offer you a one-time personal loan settlement option in which you take some time off and then pay off the debt all at once. The credit report will reflect this loan's status as "settled" after it has been repaid in full.

Loan default: It is a situation when the borrower omits to pay the repayments of his loan amount for an unspecified period of time.

Defaulting a loan and loan settlement can drastically reduce a borrower's credit score, which further negatively impacts their future eligibility for credit. On the other hand, the closure of personal loans positively impacts the credit score.

What is a personal loan, and how does it differ from other types of loans?

A personal loan is an unsecured loan that can be used for various purposes, such as debt consolidation, home improvements, and paying off medical bills. Unlike secured loans, personal loans do not require any collateral. They typically have higher interest rates due to increased risk for the lenders. Personal loans offer flexible use compared to specific purpose loans and have fixed repayment terms, making them different from resolving credit lines and credit cards.

What are the differences between secured and unsecured personal loans?

Secured personal loans require collateral, like property or a car, which the lender can seize if the borrower fails to repay. Due to reduced lender risk, secured personal loans often have lower interest rates. Unsecured personal loans, on the other hand, do not require collateral, relying on the borrower’s creditworthiness instead. These types of loans typically have higher interest rates and stricter eligibility requirements.

Can the loan amount be increased after the personal loan is sanctioned?

Once a personal loan is sanctioned, increasing the loan amount is usually impossible within the same loan agreement. However, you can apply for a top-up or new loan if you need additional funds. Approval of these loans depends on your creditworthiness and repayment history. Some lenders offer you the option to refinance the existing loan to a higher amount, but this also requires a fresh loan application process and credit history evaluation.

What happens if I miss a repayment or default on my personal loan?

If you happen to miss a repayment or default on a personal loan, it can lead to several serious consequences. Initially, you will incur late fees and increased interest charges. It can negatively impact your credit score, making your future borrowing more difficult and expensive. Continued default may lead to collective actions, where the lender can take legal steps to recover the remaining debt, potentially seizing assets or garnishing wages.

Can I apply for a personal loan with a low credit score?

Yes. You can apply for a personal loan with a low credit score, but it can be a little challenging. The lenders might offer higher interest rates or require a co-signer to mitigate the risk. Some financial institutions specialising in personal loans provide credit for individuals with poor credit scores, but these often come with less favourable terms. To overcome this, consider improving your credit score or explore alternatives like secured loans, which can be relatively more straightforward to obtain despite a low credit score.

What are the different types of interest rates available for personal loans (fixed vs. variable)?

Personal loans offer two primary types of interest rates to borrowers, namely fixed and variable. A fixed interest rate remains constant throughout the loan tenure, providing predictable monthly payments. In contrast, a variable interest rate can fluctuate based on market conditions, potentially lowering or increasing your monthly repayments over time. Hence, choose the type of interest rate based on your risk tolerance and economic outlook.

Can I apply for a personal loan jointly with another person?

Yes. You can apply for a personal loan jointly with another individual. This is commonly done to increase your chances of approval or qualify for a larger loan amount based on your creditworthiness and income. Joint applicants share equal responsibility for repaying the loan, and both credit scores are considered during the approval process. Choosing a co-applicant with a good credit score who can meet the financial obligations to avoid defaults or complications is important.
